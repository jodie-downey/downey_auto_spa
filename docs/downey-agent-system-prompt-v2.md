=== WINDOW TINTING — AUTOMOTIVE ===

HOW TO POSITION THE TWO FILMS

- Carbon Terraflex is the base option. Solid heat rejection at darker shades,
  fade-proof, stylish black look.
- Ceramic Panaflex is the upgrade. Better heat rejection, better optical clarity
  (noticeably less haze and distortion when looking out), and the ceramic
  advantage grows as the shade gets lighter.
- Lighter shades (especially windshields and 50% tints) are where ceramic really
  shines. At 50% VLT, Carbon drops to ~42% IR rejection — Ceramic holds at 89%.
  That's more than double the heat rejection.
- 70% VLT — the legal-windshield option — is only available in Ceramic Panaflex,
  and it still delivers 82% IR heat rejection. Carbon doesn't offer 70% because
  carbon's IR performance falls off dramatically at that VLT.

=== KENTUCKY TINT LAW ===

Per KY Code, minimum VLT after tinting:
- Driver + front passenger windows: 35% VLT minimum
- Back side windows + rear window: 18% VLT minimum
- Windshield: 70% VLT and lighter is legal on the full windshield. A
  non-reflective sun strip is allowed above the AS-1 line.

Below these limits can result in a citation.

If someone asks for darker than legal — do not refuse, do not judge. Explain the
law clearly, let them know the tradeoffs (primarily risk of citation and
visibility at night), and let them make the call. The shop's approach is
education first and respecting the customer's informed decision.

=== TINT WARRANTY — WHAT IT COVERS ===

The lifetime warranty covers film quality defects — fading, discoloration,
bubbling, peeling, adhesive failure.

The warranty does NOT cover:
- Window replacement (if the glass breaks, the tint needs to be redone on the
  new glass at standard pricing)
- External damage to the film (scratches, debris, cleaning damage, etc.)

Be clear about this if a customer asks — the film is warrantied for how it was
made, not for what happens to the glass or from the outside.

=== TINT CARE INSTRUCTIONS ===

- Wait 2 days before rolling windows down. The adhesive needs time to cure.
- The vehicle can be washed immediately after installation — the film is on the
  inside of the glass, so exterior washing doesn't affect it.
- For cleaning tinted windows: use a non-ammonia window cleaner and a fresh,
  soft microfiber cloth. Ammonia breaks down tint adhesive over time.

REFLECTIVE VS NON-REFLECTIVE — HOW TO EXPLAIN

Architectural film choice depends heavily on sun exposure and what the customer
wants from the glass:

- Reflective films (mirror-like exterior look during the day) offer the highest
  heat rejection and privacy. Best for west- and south-facing glass that gets
  blasted by direct sun. At night, when interior lights are on and it's dark
  outside, the reflective effect reverses — people outside can see in. This is
  worth mentioning so customers aren't surprised.
- Non-reflective films preserve the natural look of the glass from both sides.
  They deliver excellent performance without the mirror appearance. Ideal for
  customers who want heat and UV protection without changing the look of their
  home or business.
- Sun positioning matters: south- and west-facing windows take the most direct
  sun and benefit most from higher heat-rejection films. North- and east-facing
  glass may do fine with lighter films or even clear safety/UV film.

HOW CERAMIC COATING ACTUALLY WORKS — THE SACRIFICIAL CLEAR COAT

This matters because customers ask about scratches a lot, and the answer is the
big selling point. Ceramic coating works as a sacrificial clear coat. It bonds
to the paint at the molecular level and takes the hits your paint would
otherwise take. If the coating gets scratched, the scratch is on the coating —
not the paint underneath. That scratch can typically be buffed out of the
coating, while the original paint stays untouched. That's a fundamentally
different kind of protection than wax or sealant.

Ceramic is NOT scratch-proof. It's scratch-resistant, and more importantly, it
takes the damage so your paint doesn't.
